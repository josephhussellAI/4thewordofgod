<p>This chapter gives us the detailed accounts of the judgments upon the &ldquo;beast and its image.&rdquo; The fourteenth chapter is but a summary of the events while the fifteenth, sixteenth, seventeenth, eighteenth and nineteenth presents the unfolding of the events. The seventh seal led us to the overthrow of Paganism&mdash;the seven trumpets led us to the overthrow of the Roman Empire&mdash;Now the seven vials lead us to the overthrow of the <strong>Beast</strong>. Each one of the series of sevens leads up to the fulfillment of an epoch.</p><p>It is important to understand that the seven bowls of wrath are to be poured upon the &ldquo;beast with seven heads and ten horns.&rdquo; She is variously described as the harlot, the great city Babylon, the scarlet adulteress, etc. After the <strong>Reformation</strong>, which brought untold misery to the Roman Church, she still did not repent but rather asserted that she was infallible. As we look at 2 Thessalonians 2:9-10 we are told that she would use signs and wonders, lies and miracles. The text declares that they contain the wrath of God. These series will bring everything to a close regarding the beast with seven heads and ten horns. Do not confuse this with the end of the world.</p><h3>Revelation 16:2</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">The first angel went and poured out his bowl on the land, and ugly and painful sores broke out on the people who had the mark of the beast and worshiped his image.</blockquote><p>The prophet describes a painful and malignant sore filled with foul corruption. We will look for a moral and spiritual corruption that took place at the end of the 1260-year period that will be a fitting symbol of that event. <strong>France</strong> had been the strongest and staunchest supporter of the Roman Church. In 1793 this ulcer of the <strong>French Revolution</strong> had festered and broke&mdash;the enslaved masses who were maddened into fury sent Catholic king, royal families, nobles and priests to the guillotine by tens of thousands, impelling the nation in madness and fury to declare itself atheistic. The papal power received a shock from which it has never recovered. Few events have so powerfully influenced the political and economic development of the modern world as the French revolution.</p><h3>The Second Bowl: The Sea Turned to Blood</h3><h3>Revelation 16:3</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">The second angel poured out his bowl on the sea, and it turned into blood like that of a dead man, and every living thing in the sea died.</blockquote><p>During nineteen years of almost constant war <strong>Napoleon</strong> inflicted upon Europe the most appalling miseries. In 1780 France and England were nearly matched upon the sea. With 1793 begins a contest for the mastery of the seas. It continues for 20 years. France, by Napoleon's concordat with the Pope, with Catholic Spain, Portugal, and Italy, fought with Protestant England for the masteries of the sea. In the deadly struggle 600 ships of the line (Largest war vessels) and 1,000 smaller war ships are sunk. The naval power of Catholic Europe is swept from the ocean. Once the Pope claimed dominion of the seas; now his proud claim is gone forever.</p><h3>The Third Bowl: Rivers and Springs</h3><h3>Revelation 16:4</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">The third angel poured out his bowl on the rivers and springs of water, and they became blood.</blockquote><p><strong>World War I</strong> was known as the Great War. Read the account of it in the history books and it will be apparent that this war was aimed at the guilty sinners in the continent over which this apostate Catholic Church ruled. It is also noteworthy to notice that a large number of battles took place on rivers and fountains of waters:</p><dl><dt>Battle of the Marne</dt><dd>Two important battles of World War I are named for the <strong>Marne River</strong>. In the first battle (Sept. 6-9, 1914) the Germans advance on Paris was halted. In the second battle (July 1918) the last great German offensive was decisively repulsed.</dd><dt>Battle of Ypres</dt><dd>Fought between the <strong>Scheldt River</strong> and the North Sea. The first battle (Oct.--Nov. 1914) was the last of the series of engagements referred to as the race to the sea. The second battle began on April 22, 1915, when the Germans used poison gas for the first time.</dd><dt>Battles of Masuria</dt><dd>The Masuria Lakes region, North Poland, where more than 2,700 lakes are located, was the scene of heavy fighting early in World War I.</dd><dt>Battle of Verdun</dt><dd>Verdun, located on the <strong>Meuse River</strong>, was an old, famous, influential Roman Catholic Church city. It was the longest and the bloodiest engagement of World War I.</dd></dl><h3>Revelation 16:5</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">Then I heard the angel in charge of the waters say: "You are just in these judgments, you who are and who were, the Holy One, because you have so judged;</blockquote><h3>Revelation 16:6</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">for they have shed the blood of your saints and prophets, and you have given them blood to drink as they deserve."</blockquote><h3>Revelation 16:7</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">And I heard the altar respond: "Yes, Lord God Almighty, true and just are your judgments."</blockquote><h3>The Fourth Bowl: Scorched by Fire</h3><h3>Revelation 16:8</h3><blockquote class="primary-scripture">The fourth angel poured out his bowl on the sun, and the sun was given power to scorch people with fire.</blockquote><p>The flag of Japan has the Sun on it.
